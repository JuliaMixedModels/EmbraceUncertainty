    • 4 Computational Methods for Mixed Models

    In this chapter we describe some of the details of the computational methods for fitting linear mixed models, as implemented in the package, and the theoretical development behind these methods. We also provide the basis for later generalizations to models for non-Gaussian responses and to models in which the relationship between the conditional mean, \(\mathbf{\mu}\), and the linear predictor, \(\mathbf{\gamma}=\mathbf{X}\mathbf{\beta}+\mathbf{Z}\mathbf{b}= \mathbf{Z}\Lambda_\theta\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{X}\mathbf{\beta}\), is a nonlinear relationship.

    This material is directed at those readers who wish to follow the theory and methodology of linear mixed models and how both can be extended to other forms of mixed models. Readers who are less interested in the “how” and the “why” of fitting mixed models than in the results themselves should not feel obligated to master these details.
